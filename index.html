Understanding Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

An eigenvector is a non-zero vector that only changes by a scalar factor when a linear transformation is applied to it. The corresponding scalar is called the eigenvalue.

For a matrix A, eigenvector v and eigenvalue λ satisfy:

Av = λv

Drag vectors in the visualization to see how they transform. Eigenvectors will remain on their span during transformation.
